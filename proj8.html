This assignment asks you to produce an interface for a program that will play Othello along with a short and informal (for now) description of the structure of your system. The completed game will be your final examination.

Produce an interface for Othello that conforms to the following rules, specifications, and conventions.

  • The interface may be text-based or a GUI

Specifications for interaction.

Though the game will have no AI yet, I'll expect to be able to go through the sequence of moves described below.

P = Player
A = AI Player
S = System that keeps track of things
See "Conventions," below for details on the look of the board and other important items.

Play takes place on two computers. Suppose Team 1's A makes a move. Team 2 enters that move on its computer. Now Team 2's A makes a move. Team 1 enters that move on its computer. Continue the process until play concludes.

Specifically:

  1. S displays the initial board configuration. Scoreboard indicates that both black and white have a score of 2.

  2. S asks P if he/she wants to be black or white (the teams have previously determined between themselves who will go first, i.e., who will be black)

  3. S reads P's answer. If P chooses black, go to step 12.

  4. If P chooses white, A announces that it is about to make a move and asks P for approval.

  5. S reads P's answer.

  6. S displays the position where A has placed a token in row/column format. This can be done in any way you choose, as long as it's clear.

  7. If A can't make a move, A announces that fact.

  8. The board configuration now includes the new token (if one has been played). The square where the move occurred along with all squares affected by the move are highlighted.

  9. S asks for confirmation.

  10. S reads P ’s answer.

  11. After P has confirmed, S changes the board configuration and the scoreboard to reflect the results of the new move.

  12. S asks P to make a move.

  13. S reads P ’s answer.

  14. The board configuration now includes the new token. The square where the move occurred along with all squares affected by the move are highlighted.

  15. S asks for confirmation.

  16. After A has confirmed (actually, the user must confirm for A), S changes the board configuration and the scoreboard to reflect the results of the new move.

  17. Continue this process until P quits by pressing a designated key or until there are no more moves.

Conventions and Additional Rules

  • The board is an 8 x 8 matrix with an initial configuration like this:
    WB
    BW

  • Your game allows the initial configuration to be changed to:
    BW
    WB

  • Columns are labeled ‘A’ to ‘H’ from left to right. Rows are labeled ‘1’ to ‘8’ from top to bottom. The system must display move coordinates

  • Designate squares like this

    Black occupies a square: B or place a black shape on the square

    White occupies a square: W or place a white shape on the square

  • There is a score board that displays the number of black and the number of white tokens on the board.

  • Black goes first. Players choose who is black.

  • Your system may not decline to move if one is available

  • Each system must display a timer that ticks off seconds starting from 10 when a move is initiated. A system whose AI fails to move within 10 seconds forfeits the game.

  • Your system must always save the prior board state and designate a mechanism by which it can be returned to in the event of a dispute

  • Play ends when neither player can make a move. The player with the most discs wins.
